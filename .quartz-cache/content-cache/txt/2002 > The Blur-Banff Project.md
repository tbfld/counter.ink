In 2002, I was part of the planning group — along with [Robert Ransick](https://robertransick.com/), [Katie Salen](https://www.katiesalen.me/about), [Marina Zurkow](https://o-matic.com/), and a few others — for the conference Blur 02: Power at Play in Digital Art and Culture, co-developed by NYC’s arts org [Creative Time](https://creativetime.org/) and the [New School University](https://www.newschool.edu/). (Visible in the CT photo: Clay Shirky, Meg Hourihan, who went on to found the ur-blogging company Blogger), and [Natalie Jeremijenko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natalie_Jeremijenko), among others.) At the time, my constellation of interests was pretty unusual — notably European arts activism and US telecoms policy — so I brought an unusual group of people to the conference, among them the activists [Alan Toner](https://wiki.p2pfoundation.net/Alan_Toner) and [James Love](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Love_%28NGO_director%29). With [Jamie J. King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_King_(producer)) and [Richie Hawtin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richie_Hawtin) (who then worked under the name Plastikman), we formed a group that sought to radically rethink the relationship between music and money at the dawn of P2P sharing. [Sara Diamond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sara_Diamond_(academic_administrator)), an important curator at the [Banff Centre](https://www.banffcentre.ca/) in Canada later invited us there to further develop and present the idea. Jamie Love’s documentation of the proposal and its later circuitous influence can be found here:

- “[The Blur Banff proposal](https://www.keionline.org/blur-banff)” (2013)
- “[Blur 02 | Power at Play in Digital Art and Culture (April 11-13, 2002) User Experience](https://keionline.org/wp-content/uploads/02-04_Love_blur-banff.pdf)” (at Blur, 2002)
- “[How should musicians be paid to create digital works?](https://keionline.org/sites/default/files/banff-p2p-cl.pdf)” (at Banff, 2002)
- “[An Essential Health Care Patent Pool](https://keionline.org/sites/default/files/jameslove-barcelona.pdf)” (Barcelona, 2002)
