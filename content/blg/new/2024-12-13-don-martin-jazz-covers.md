A multi-FOAF just named Marc Campbell [posted](https://www.facebook.com/marc.campbell/posts/pfbid0exH7ys6LSL9rbHN5nxe3rKSq2qyFit5BQDSBezji1AKzbgomuupGR1uHAtL58pfFl) some album covers designed for the [Prestige](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prestige_Records) label by [the cartoonist Don Martin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Martin_(cartoonist)), who AFAIK (and I know pretty far) is known almost exclusively for his work for [*Mad* magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mad_(magazine)). His *Mad* cartoons — hyperstylized people, simple compositions, all outline no texture,  etc — became so established that it’s astonishing to see drawings that show signs of [Edward Gorey](https://www.edwardgoreyhouse.org/) or, more obscurely, [Irving Geis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irving_Geis). There might be more in a 2009 book that bills itself plainly as *[Prestige Records: The Album Cover Collection](http://isbn.nu/0615318363)*, but coffee-table collections like that tend to be more curatorial than completist, so it’s easy to imagine that Martin’s designs — if there are any more — might drop out.
