A friend shared a meme about student debt that pointed out something obvious but rarely noted. Many borrowers have long since paid off the principle and even quite a bit of interest; the debt they continue to struggle under is often just a result of the spread between sane and insane interest rates. My reply:

> There are all kinds of legit age-related criticisms to be made about Biden, but it's a *fact* that he was like a pit bull when it came to reducing educational debt -- he would. not. let. go. Setback after setback, he chipped away at it. The problem was that he only *chipped*, when he could and should have waved his magic wand and said "SHAZAM! NO SUCH THING ANYMORE!" -- especially after the Supreme Court issue that insane immunity ruling. And, if anything, this very issue really exemplifies the tragic aspect of his presidency: his age-related inability to communicate effectively -- from his nostalgic faith in a long-gone public sphere that would clearly convey his policies, to his inner circle's growing efforts to hide him away -- he never got much credit for this, one of his main most consistent policies, and one that could and should have spoken clearly to younger generations.
