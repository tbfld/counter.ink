[This is super-boring eduwonk and also very true and important.](https://www.chronicle.com/article/a-crusade-against-terrible-advising) The tl;dr: if higher ed changed its approach to advising students from alienated compliance to something much more individual and challenging, it would have a *huge* and positive impact. But there are very few immediate efficiencies and no naming opportunities in talking with students as individuals in their own right.
