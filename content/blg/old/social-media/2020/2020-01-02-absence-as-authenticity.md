“semantically tortured” seems like a useful phrase. [This essay](https://thereader.mitpress.mit.edu/the-myth-of-a-wilderness-without-humans/) is a weird combination of interesting and boring, but what strikes me most of all is how often it relies on the *absence* of a word as a sign of authenticity: “they ** have no word for**.” If the author really believed this, the only way to speak the truth would be silence.
