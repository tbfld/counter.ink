Help me out here, people. The cartoon on the left is a classic, which Mr. Internet says *In These Times* ran in ‘89. That seems a about right, time-wise. I want to ask what the hell kind of person would look at it and say “That’s hilarious, but it’ll be so. much. better when I redo it artlessly!” That invites a psychological view, but — in keeping with the subject (“But of course!”) — the answer probably spans several disciplines. This kind of thing happens *all the time*. Why do people do this? How could someone look at the one on the right and think “Now *that* is how it should be!”?
