VERY interesting: the agency that runs Tallahassee’s public transit system is running [a public survey that gives you an imaginary $100 to allocate across a range of options](https://starmetrothinktransit.com/). It’s a really smart way to walk the public into an overview of the issues and tradeoffs as well as work up some pretty rich data. It preserves the zero-sum logic of policy choices, but what strikes me is how it quietly ditches *binary* either/or models. It’s beaten into our heads that progress = democracy = voting, but it’s not necessarily so. For various reasons, elections more and more take the form of beauty contests between self-styled visionaries, but mainly what we need are *stewards* — people who can be trusted to shepherd specific social values. The importance of this distinction became clear in the last US election, when (remember?) it was professional civil servants — USPS and election workers — who, collectively, saved our collective ass. Take a moment to imagine a US federal election that looked like this survey: you get an imaginary $100 and you’re asked to spread it across a variety of issues lumped under categories like domestic law enforcement, foreign relations, education, health and safety, business and commerce, the young and old, social equality and advancement, the environment, infrastructure and technology, quality of life...
