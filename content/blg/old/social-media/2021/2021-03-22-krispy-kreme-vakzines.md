Not interested in [the Krispy Kreme angle](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/krispy-kreme-free-doughnut-every-day-2021-covid-19-vaccination-card/) (PLZ, I’m a proper son of SoCal and can’t stand their crap). But I *am* interested in the roles that corps have played and can play in solving this mess. For ex, when big boxes like Home Depot adopted mask policies, *that*, far more govt policy at any level, set the standard that smaller retailers needed to follow nationally. KK offering a free donut *any or every day* for the rest of the year is pretty interesting: it’s an ongoing incentive, it’s simple, and it respects privacy (no data collected or stored). Oh, and in a very Southern chain not exactly associated with health. If more places — let’s say Chick-fil-A or Waffle Hut — thought about how they could drive vaccine acceptance, it could make a real difference. So props to Krispy Kreme for this.
