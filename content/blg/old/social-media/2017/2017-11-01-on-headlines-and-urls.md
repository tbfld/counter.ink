This is a great window into the kinds of discussions involved in how the news should cope with the problem of ‘fake news.’ On the left is the *NYT*’s front page, on the right is the page it links to. Fox has been faking news for decades, nothing new there. What *is* new, I think, is that the Murdoch machine will foment civil war before it’ll rethink its position. It’s not “failing to unite a nation,” it’s actively working to destroy the US government. If that sounds like an exaggeration, what’s a better way to describe things like advocating the arrest of losing candidates and a putsch in the Justice Department? So the *NYT* names names on the front page — that’s great “clickbait” — and equivocates on the story page. The tell is in the neither/nor URL: “russia-presidential-election-media.html.”
