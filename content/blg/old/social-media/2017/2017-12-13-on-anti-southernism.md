One reason — one of many — I’ve become Epipen-level allergic to [yankee anti-southern arrogance](https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2017/12/despite-the-obstacles-black-voters-make-a-statement-in-alabama/548237/?utm*source=atlfb) is that it casts all southerns as white. This supposed whiteness of the south is itself a legacy of white supremacy. When you think about how snarky stereotypes about “the south” might sound across the spectrum of people t/here, you’ll see one reason — of many — that it needs to stop. We need an image of the south that’s founded on respect for its diversity and sees *that* as its future.
