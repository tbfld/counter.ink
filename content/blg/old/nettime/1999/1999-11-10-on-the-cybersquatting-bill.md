Felix Stalder (Wed 11/10/99 at 02:41 PM -0500):

> This is vintage politics. A major issue is buried in a bill that has nothing to do with it but is unlikely to be vetoed because then the whole bill would have to be vetoed. This case it’s the a piece of legislation lobbied by the Motion Pictures Association that gives trademark holders more right to sue alleged “cybersquatters”. And the vehicle in which this law was passed was the “Satellite Viewers Act” which nominally regulates cable vs. satellite TV issues. The has just passed the Senate and now goes on to the House.
